ICML 2025 Workshop on 2nd Open-World Agents

April 27, 2025 in EXPO, Singapore

Links: Twitter | OpenReview | ICLR 2025

ICML 2025 Workshop on 2nd Open-World Agents explores the development of AI agents with emergent capabilities that surpass human performance in specific tasks, a topic that has significantly expanded the potential for AI in open-world environments. Building on the rapid progress of foundation models, we focus on agents that can dynamically perceive environmental information, perform long-horizon task decomposition or reasoning, make decisions, continuously learn to build world models, use tools, and interact with other agents through competition or collaboration. These capabilities are seen as a key pathway toward artificial general intelligence. The workshop aims to bring together researchers to discuss these topics and their applications in areas like robotics and workflow automation, emphasizing the need for universal methodologies and interdisciplinary insights.

Previous Workshop: ICLR 2024 Workshop on LLM Agents

Topics

We will explore a range of topics in this workshop, including, but not limited to, the following areas:

Learning:

This section will focus on the development of scalable learning objectives to perform complex tasks with effective supervision of their reasoning and decision-making processes. It will also involve defining and constructing world models tailored to various open-world environments, and leveraging these models to empower agents. Additionally, it will explore integrating multimodal learning to enable agents to reason over diverse sensory inputs, as well as constructing reward models for continuous improvements in open-ended scenarios.

Data:

This section will address the development of efficient data collection strategies in real or simulated environments to train foundation models with diverse capabilities. It will also include an investigation into the quantity, quality, and diversity of training datasets and their impact on generalization in open-world settings.

Evaluation:

This section will focus on establishing evaluation protocols that closely replicate and imply real-world consequences.

New Architecture and System Design:

This section will delve into establishing a unified methodology for designing agent architectures and developing criteria to assess their quality. It will also involve building multi-agent learning frameworks in open-world environments where agents can autonomously collaborate, and establishing evaluation criteria for multi-agent systems.

Submission Tracks:

Consistent with the themes of the workshop, we invite contributions in the areas highlighted above. However, we emphasize that the topics list is not exhaustive and welcome submissions in related areas. There is no need to specify your track on OpenReview. Our workshop will not accept work that has been previously published in other conferences on machine learning. Work that is presented at the main ICLR conference should not be submitted to us as well.

  • Research Paper Track: We welcome a variety of original research papers, including but not limited to those that propose new techniques, discussion-based papers, literature surveys, and position papers. Research papers can have a maximum length of up to 9 pages of content, plus unlimited pages for references and appendix.
  • Demo Paper Track: We also welcome technical reports for the demo track, with a maximum of 9 pages (same as research papers). In addition to the paper, please provide a link to a video, website, or code repository showcasing your demo.
